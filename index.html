WHERE THIS BEGAN

In 1913, cartoonist Alfred Frueh sent his wife Giuliette a love letter that unfolded into a tiny art gallery — paintings on the walls, a coat-check sign, her name on the door.

It still exists today, preserved in the Smithsonian's Archives of American Art. This site is our small tribute to that letter — credit always to Alfred Frueh.
